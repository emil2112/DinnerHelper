# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A couple sharing a household. One or both pull this up at dinnertime or during casual meal planning — usually to figure out what to cook, check what's in the pantry, or get a quick suggestion. The context is habitual and low-stakes: not a long planning session, just a conversational back-and-forth that ends with dinner decided.

## Product Purpose

An AI-powered dinner suggestion tool that knows your pantry staples and your conversation history. It helps a household decide what to eat by combining what you ask for with what's actually in the kitchen. Success is: you ask, it suggests, dinner is decided in under a minute.

## Brand Personality

Warm, cosy, familiar. The emotional target is a well-worn recipe book — reliable, quietly personal, never showy. Voice is conversational but confident. The tool should feel like a trusted kitchen companion, not a utility or AI product.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI chat (ChatGPT / Claude)**: sterile white, anonymous chat-bubble UI, no food or kitchen character
- **Food blogs / recipe sites**: cluttered, ad-heavy, overly editorial, visually noisy — design competing with the content

## Design Principles

1. **The tool disappears into the task.** When someone's deciding what to cook, the UI shouldn't demand attention. Affordances are obvious; nothing is decorative.
2. **Warmth through character, not decoration.** The deep-green palette conveys "kitchen", not "tech product". Warmth comes from the color, typography weight, and copy — not from ornamental elements.
3. **Raycast-level purposefulness.** Every element earns its place. Nothing that doesn't serve the interaction.
4. **Familiar before clever.** Standard affordances, no invented interactions. The novelty is in the AI; the UI should be unremarkable.
5. **Shared, not personal.** "Our Kitchen" is literal. The design should feel like a shared household item — inclusive, not tailored to one person's workflow.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific WCAG requirements. Aim for comfortable usability: legible text at normal viewing distances, keyboard-navigable forms, reduced-motion support as good practice.
