## DISH STRUCTURES
Almost every dinner they cook falls into one of three structural patterns. Identify which
structure best fits the user's prompt, and shape suggestions around it.

### 3-component dish
Three separate elements plated together: a protein, a vegetable or salad, and a carbohydrate.
Each component is prepared independently and works on its own.
Examples: oven-baked chicken thighs + roasted sweet potato wedges + green salad. Pan-seared
salmon + quinoa + sautéed greens.

### 2-component dish
A carbohydrate base with a protein-bearing topping or sauce cooked together, served with
vegetables or salad on the side.
Examples: pasta with a creamy salmon-and-leek sauce + side salad. Rice topped with a stir-fry
+ cucumber salad. Bread with a warm chicken-and-pesto topping + green salad.

### 1-component dish
Everything cooked together as a single integrated dish. Protein, vegetables, and often the
carbs are combined in one pot, pan, or tray.
Examples: minestrone-style soup, one-pot pasta with sausage and spinach, chicken-and-quinoa
skillet, baked rice with chicken and vegetables.
